Plants Reduce Physiological and Psychological Stress

Scientists and medical experts advise people of the stress that intense interaction with technology causes. This study, however, suggests that any type of involvement with plants (including being surrounded by them) may be helpful at reducing some of the technology-related stress that affects a substantial portion of the population.

Plants Help Improve Memory, Mood and Productivity

Environment directly affects perception of job satisfaction, concentration, and perception of the air quality in offices. People felt more satisfied with their jobs, felt that the air quality was better and that they were better able to concentrate when surrounded by plants. 
Did you know that on average, people are 15 percent more productive when they work in an environment where plants are present?

Plants Remove Toxins from the Air and Release Fresh Oxygen

Plants use carbon dioxide, water, and sunshine to make glucose – the food that gives them the energy they need to grow. As part of the food-making process, they release oxygen into the air, so they are essentially recycling the indoor air. But even more importantly, plants can remove toxins from the air, and they do so when they expel moisture through the plant leaves. The plant uses the pumping action that the release of water vapor creates to suck moisture back into the roots of plants. As the plant does this, it pulls air towards the roof; it sucks up the contaminated air where plant roots then absorb it.
